### The Trust Formation Cycle

What Mercy did â€” whether she knows it or not â€” is a textbook case of how trust forms in communities where institutions have repeatedly failed.

It follows a pattern: peer recommendation first. One person tells another. Then a small test â€” Mercy converted a small amount, showed the women it worked. Then community adoption â€” other savings clubs in Harare started asking Mercy how she did it.

That nervousness is universal. Every new financial technology starts with someone afraid of pressing the wrong button. What separates adoption from abandonment is whether the first experience works. For Mercy's club, it did. For the M-Pesa agent, it did. The nervous moment passed, and the behavior stuck.

This pattern â€” trust formed through experience rather than granted through authority â€” explains why stablecoins are spreading fastest in places where institutional trust is lowest.
