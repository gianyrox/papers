## SECTION 3: PROGRAMMABLE MONEY — WHAT BECOMES POSSIBLE

### 3A: Smart Contract Automation
- Programmable payroll: salary splits automatically (rent, savings, investments, charity) at the moment of payment
- Conditional payments: escrow that releases on delivery, milestone-based freelance payments, insurance that pays out automatically when conditions met
- DAO treasuries: decentralized organizations paying contributors globally in stablecoins with governance-voted budgets
- Revenue sharing: smart contracts that split incoming payments among multiple parties in real-time (music royalties, business partnerships)
- "Money with rules built in": government aid that can only be spent at approved merchants, child support that goes to children's expenses

### 3B: Tokenized Everything (the 2030 Vision)
- BlackRock's Larry Fink on tokenization: "the next generation for markets"
- Tokenized stocks, bonds, real estate: settled in stablecoins
- What does a fully tokenized financial system look like?
- The 24/7 market: no market close, no settlement delays, global access
- Fractional ownership: $10 of Apple stock, $50 of a Manhattan apartment
- Citi, Standard Chartered, Bernstein projections for tokenized asset market size by 2030
- How stablecoins serve as the "cash leg" of every tokenized transaction

### 3C: Programmable Money for Governments
- Tax collection via smart contracts (automatic withholding at point of transaction)
- Government aid with built-in conditions (spendable only at pharmacies, grocery stores)
- Real-time GDP measurement via on-chain transaction data
- Budget transparency: every government dollar traceable on-chain
- Counter-argument: surveillance state risks, over-programming money (China social credit parallels)
