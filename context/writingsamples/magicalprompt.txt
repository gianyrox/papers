This guide will show you the magic of bringing your inspirations to life in minutes. However, your app will get better and better the more time you devote to it. So use the strategies you learn in this guide to iterate on your one-shot prompt app and truly build something magical!

1. Set up the project directory
Open your terminal and run:

mkdir [appname]
cd [appname]
Now open the folder in Cursor (https://cursor.sh).

2. Prompt AI to generate the app
In Cursor, press Ctrl + L to enter chat mode. Then paste the following prompt:

Create an app named [appname] that does [appdescription]  
branding - [describe the look, feel, tone, and colors of the brand]  
users & niche - [who is this app for and what specific need does it serve?]  
stack - [preferred tech stack or keep it minimal if unsure]  
core features - [list 3–5 main features the app should have]  
platform - [web / mobile / desktop]  
auth - [does the app require login? if yes, what kind—email, social, etc.]  
data & storage - [describe what kind of data the app will handle and if a database is needed]  
git for tracking (GitHub)  
log for testing
The goal here is to provide the AI with vector-rich context so it can generate a clean, minimal, and well-structured app that fits your needs. Keep the stack as simple as possible. For example, a landing page might only require an index.html.

3. Follow the AI’s instructions
Send the prompt and carefully read through the response.

Run all terminal commands provided by the AI. You don’t have to understand every line of code, but pay attention to the system architecture and the steps the AI is taking. Mentally track the terminal commands so you can learn how to replicate them solo.

4. Run and test the app
Let the AI provide the start/run commands.

If you encounter errors:

Copy the error message.
Paste it back into the chat.
Include the file or context mentioned in the error.
Repeat this loop as needed. If the chat starts looping or struggling, pause and read back through the context. Make sure you’re in the right thread. If you can’t find your app logs, ask the AI where the logs are located for your app.

5. Make updates
Updating your app works just like building the first version.

Remember, the AI performs best with clean slates. So if you’re updating a page, component, or subdirectory, treat it as its own small, isolated project.

Become a member
It helps to make codebase-wide helper directories or files:

@types  
@colors  
@styles
These provide reusable and consistent context for your updates.

6. Deploy
For websites:
The simplest deployment workflow is:

GitHub (host) → Vercel (deployment) → Namecheap (DNS)
Connect your repo to Vercel and follow their easy deployment flow. Then point your domain to Vercel through your Namecheap DNS settings.

For mobile apps:
iOS (Apple App Store)

Enroll in the Apple Developer Program ($99/year).
Prepare your app and test with Xcode and TestFlight.
Create your App Store Connect record at App Store Connect.
Fill in app metadata and screenshots.
Set pricing and availability.
Archive and upload the build using Xcode.
Submit your app for review.
Reference: Apple’s official iOS submission guide

Android (Google Play Store)

Create a Google Play Developer Account ($25 one-time).
Test and prepare your app with Android Studio.
Generate a signed Android App Bundle (.aab).
Set up your app listing in the Google Play Console.
Fill out the content rating and policy declarations.
Upload the .aab and submit for review.
Reference: Google Play app publishing guide

7. Iterate, learn, and share
From here you can iterate on your app, learn with a new app, or share your app!

1. Iterate your app. Configure your development environment to automatically:

save changes → run build checks → git add → git commit → git push
This lets you go live in minutes after writing code. You want to shorten the gap between idea and live update just like gap between idea and live app.

The technology for shortening this gap is getting better and better. Soon you will be able to update with voice commands on your phone — picture walking in nature while building and deploying your app.

2. Learn by building a new app. If you want to deepen your skills, just restart this guide with a new idea. Each app is a chance to learn something new.

3. Share your app. Let people use it. Get feedback. Iterate again or move on to your next build.
